Fox Kitten is threat actor with a suspected nexus to the Iranian government that has been active since at least 2017 against entities in the Middle East, North Africa, Europe, Australia, and North America. Fox Kitten has targeted multiple industrial verticals including oil and gas, technology, government, defense, healthcare, manufacturing, and engineering. Fox Kitten has accessed ntuser.dat and UserClass.dat on compromised hosts. Fox Kitten has used 7-Zip to archive data. Fox Kitten has brute forced RDP credentials. Fox Kitten has used PowerShell scripts to access credential data. Fox Kitten has used a Perl reverse shell to communicate with C2. Fox Kitten has used scripts to access credential information from the KeePass database. Fox Kitten has created KeyBase accounts to communicate with ransomware victims. Fox Kitten has used sticky keys to launch a command prompt. Fox Kitten has downloaded additional tools including PsExec directly to endpoints. Fox Kitten has used Volume Shadow Copy to access credential information from NTDS.
